The 1-page marketing plan for Adelaide small businesses

By the lemonlolly team·8 July 2026·6 min read

Most marketing plans die in a drawer. They're forty pages long, full of words like "synergy," and by the time you've finished writing one, the quarter's over. Local businesses don't need a novel — they need one page they'll actually look at.

This is the exact one-page plan we build with every lemonlolly client. It forces five decisions, fits on a single sheet, and takes about an hour to fill in. Here's how it works.

Why one page beats forty

A plan you re-read every week is worth more than a perfect strategy you write once and forget. Constraint is the feature here: if it doesn't fit on a page, it won't survive a busy month. The one-pager keeps you honest about the handful of things that actually move the needle for a local business.

The five boxes

Every version of the plan answers the same five questions, in order. The first one is the one most businesses skip — and it's the one everything else hangs off.

1. Who exactly is this for?

Not "everyone in Adelaide." One specific customer — their suburb, their budget, the problem they're trying to solve at 9pm on their phone. Write them as a real person.

2. What do you want them to do?

One primary action per campaign: book, call, buy, or visit. If you're chasing three actions at once, you'll get none.

3. Where will you reach them?

Pick two channels you can actually keep up with — not all of them. For most local businesses that's Instagram plus Google Business Profile, or Meta ads plus email.

4. What will you say?

Your one-line promise, plus three proof points. This becomes the backbone of every post, ad and page.

5. How will you know it worked?

One number that matters — bookings, enquiries, foot traffic — checked on the same day each week. Everything else is a vanity metric.
