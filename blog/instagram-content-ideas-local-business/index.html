15 Instagram content ideas for local Adelaide businesses

By the lemonlolly team·8 July 2026·5 min read

The hardest part of Instagram isn't the algorithm — it's staring at an empty content calendar on a Monday morning wondering what on earth to post. So here's a cheat sheet: fifteen ideas any local Adelaide business can shoot on a phone this week. No studio, no agency, no excuses.

But first — a quick reason it's worth the twenty minutes.

Why bother? The numbers
90%

of Instagram users follow at least one business account

200M

80%

Sources: BrightLocal & published Instagram business statistics, 2025–26.

Translation: your future customers are already on Instagram and already Googling local businesses like yours. The only question is whether they find something worth following when they land on your profile.

Behind-the-scenes (the stuff people actually love)

Your customers are nosy in the best way — they want to see how the sausage gets made. Show them.

  • The morning setup. Opening up, prepping, the first coffee of the day. Quiet, human, real.
  • Meet the maker. A 15-second clip of a team member introducing themselves and what they do.
  • A tour of your space. Walk the phone through the shop, kitchen, or studio.
  • The bloopers. The cake that collapsed, the order that went sideways. Imperfection builds trust.

Show your work

Proof beats promises every single time. Let the results do the talking.

  • Before & after. The single highest-performing format for almost every local service business.
  • A finished piece, up close. Slow pans and detail shots — texture sells.
  • A customer reaction. With permission, capture the moment someone sees the result.

“People don't scroll past a business they feel like they know. Post like a human, not a brochure — that's the whole trick, and it's free.”

IndyFounder, lemonlolly

Be genuinely useful

Teach one small thing and you become the local expert people remember. It's also how you show up in search — helpful content is what Google and Instagram both reward.

  • A quick how-to. One tip, one problem solved, under 30 seconds.
  • Myth-busting. "No, you don't actually need to…" — corrections perform brilliantly.
  • A local recommendation. Shout out another Adelaide business. Goodwill travels.

Quick tip

Film everything vertical (9:16) and keep the first second punchy — that's the frame that decides whether someone keeps watching or scrolls past. And make sure your Google Business Profile is filled in too, so the people who find you on Instagram can also find you on Maps.

Bring people in

Community is the whole point of "social" media.

  • A question sticker. "What should we make next?" People love being asked.
  • Reshare a customer's post. Free content and social proof in one.
  • A this-or-that poll. Two options, one tap. Effortless engagement.
  • A local landmark or event. Tie your post to something Adelaide — Fringe, a footy weekend, a heatwave.
  • The "regular order." Feature a loyal customer and their usual. Everyone wants to be the regular.
“

Marketing is no longer about the stuff you make, but about the stories you tell.

— Seth Godin, author of Purple Cow

That's the mindset behind every idea on this list. You're not "doing social media" — you're telling the ongoing story of a local business people want to root for. None of it needs to be perfect. The businesses that win on Instagram aren't the ones with the best camera; they're the ones that show up consistently and sound like a human. Start with three this week.
